The Problem (From Actual Data)

After training 55+ startups,
six patterns kept repeating.

Every data point below is real — pulled from mentor scorecards, startup interviews, and post-event debriefs across CES and VivaTech cohorts.

PATTERN 1

The Supplier Trap

Founders lead with factory credentials — yield rate, BOM, ODM — instead of user problems. Hardware startups settle for being a "supplier to a supplier" instead of building direct buyer relationships.

PATTERN 2

Communication Failures

CEO pitches in limited English while a fluent team member watches silently. "Good tech with terrible storytelling" — Upbeat Technology scored 1/5. Feature dumping instead of problem-solution narrative.

PATTERN 3

Market & Strategy Blindness

"Reconnecting with Disney" as an entire US strategy. "Completely unaware of competitors" in crowded fields. No GDPR awareness. Using investor decks for buyer meetings.

PATTERN 4

Digital Hygiene Failures

No LinkedIn presence. Wix sites for B2B companies. Chinese-only websites while planning US trade shows. Gmail addresses instead of company domains. Conflicting company names across platforms.

PATTERN 5

Sales & Lead Conversion

300+ CES visitors but "don't know how to convert into customers." Lost touch with all leads after returning to Taiwan. No pre-show outreach — rely on walk-up traffic only. No CRM.

PATTERN 6

Coachability Gaps

One mentor's entire feedback: "Ability to listen" — scored 1/5. Sent junior IT staff to strategy interviews. No-shows for scheduled workshops. One-person operations at scale.

Key Exercises

What happens in the room.

The Forbidden Words Drill

Pitch with banned supplier vocabulary (yield rate, BOM, ODM, factory, cost-down). Required user vocabulary (driver, passenger, experience, pain point, time saved). Buzzer on violations. Restart from zero.

The Technical Inquisition

Once you can pitch user value, mentors shift to hostile technical due diligence. "You claim 40% improvement. Prove it. Data. Now." Say "we think" or "we plan to" — meeting over.

The Communication Shift

Round 1: CEO forbidden from speaking. Round 2: CEO gets 30 seconds, communicator does everything else. Round 3: No slides, no notes. Round 4: Mentor switches persona mid-pitch.

The Lead Sprint

50 qualified contacts. Outreach emails drafted, mentor-reviewed, and sent before anyone leaves the building. Not hypothetical. Real emails to real buyers on Show Day.
